Basic AI System Characteristics

Article 3 AI Act — Definition of 'AI system'
System Type

Which of the following best describes your system?

Select the category that best fits the core technology of your AI. This helps determine if the AI Act applies at all and which category of rules is relevant.

📖 Under Art. 3(1) AI Act, an AI system is a machine-based system that operates with some degree of autonomy and infers outputs (predictions, decisions, content, recommendations) influencing real or virtual environments. Traditional deterministic software generally falls outside this definition.
4

General-Purpose AI Model Details

Articles 51–55 AI Act — GPAI Classification
GPAI Classification

Does your GPAI model have systemic risk?

A GPAI model is presumed to have systemic risk if it was trained using more than 10²⁵ FLOPs or if the European Commission designates it as such (Art. 51). This triggers additional obligations.

💡 If systemic risk is uncertain, it is recommended to treat the model as if it has systemic risk to ensure compliance. The AI Office may also designate GPAI models as systemic risk.

Regulated Product Integration

Article 6(1) & Annex I AI Act
High-Risk (Annex I)

Is your AI system a safety component of, or itself a product regulated under, one of these EU laws?

If your AI is used as a safety component in — or is itself — a product covered by EU harmonisation legislation requiring third-party conformity assessment, it is automatically high-risk (Art. 6(1)).

High-Risk Use Case Domains

Article 6(2) & Annex III AI Act
High-Risk Domains (Annex III)

In which domain(s) is your AI system primarily used?

Select all that apply. AI systems used in these domains are generally classified as high-risk under Annex III of the AI Act (unless the Art. 6(3) exemption applies).

⚠️ Even if your system falls in one of these domains, it may not be high-risk if it only performs a narrow procedural task, improves a previously completed human activity, or is purely preparatory (Art. 6(3)). However, profiling of individuals always qualifies as high-risk.
